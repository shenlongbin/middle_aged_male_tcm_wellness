# Requirements

## Project Background

The project targets educated middle-aged men, primarily Chinese 75s/80s generation professionals, who increasingly experience fatigue, poor sleep, body composition decline, chronic-risk indicators, stress, and anxiety about aging. These users are dissatisfied with both low-trust health marketing and overly narrow disease-only explanations.

The opportunity is to create a high-trust content and service brand that starts from modern medical and lifestyle logic, then bridges into TCM-informed wellness without anti-science rhetoric.

## Target User

Primary user:

- Male
- Age 38 to 55
- Urban China
- College-educated or above
- Middle-class or upper-middle-class income
- Often works or worked in large companies, professional services, tech, finance, manufacturing management, or public institutions

User traits:

- Understands health check reports at a basic level
- Familiar with terms like脂肪肝, 血脂高, 尿酸高, 睡不好, 焦虑, 免疫力下降
- Feels modern medicine can diagnose disease but not fully solve daily sub-health
- Rejects low-end elderly marketing and exaggerated cure claims
- Wants health management that feels intelligent, practical, and dignified

## User Problem

Primary problem:

Users feel their body state is declining, but they do not have a trusted, coherent framework that connects symptoms, habits, aging anxiety, and practical recovery routines.

Common manifestations:

- Sleep becomes shallow or fragmented
- Recovery is slower after work, stress, meals, or drinking
- Weight and abdominal fat increase
- Energy, libido, and motivation drop together
- Health check indicators trend worse over time
- Existing health content feels too shallow, too mystical, or too sales-driven

## Product Objective

Create a content-led MVP that helps the target user understand and act on sub-health and wellness decline through a modern, trustworthy, TCM-aware framework.

## Core Value Proposition

Use modern health logic to explain why middle-aged men feel they are declining, then provide practical, low-shame, TCM-informed wellness guidance they can actually use.

## Product Form for MVP

Phase-1 MVP should be a content website or landing experience with:

- Clear positioning
- Structured topic clusters
- Trust-building long-form content
- Email or waitlist capture
- Feedback or consultation interest capture

The MVP is not a diagnosis platform and not a treatment app.

## Feature List

P0:

- Homepage with positioning and user resonance
- Problem-solution framing for the target audience
- 3 to 5 pillar content sections
- At least 3 detailed content pages or modules
- Email or lead capture form
- Feedback/contact path

P1:

- Self-assessment quiz for "body state"
- Structured 7-day starter plan
- Private community waitlist
- Content tagging by topic such as sleep, stress,脂肪肝,体能恢复

P2:

- Paid course landing page
- Member plan comparison
- Case-story library
- Expert collaboration page

## User Flow

1. User discovers a piece of content through social, search, or community sharing.
2. User lands on homepage or article page.
3. User recognizes a familiar problem pattern.
4. User reads a modern explanation plus a TCM-informed interpretation.
5. User receives a simple next-step action or starter plan.
6. User opts into follow-up through email, waitlist, or consultation interest.

## Page and Module List

- Homepage
- About / positioning section
- Pillar page: sleep and recovery
- Pillar page: metabolic warning signs
- Pillar page: stress and aging anxiety
- Pillar page: TCM concepts translated for modern users
- Starter plan page
- Contact / waitlist page

## Metrics To Track

- Landing page visit volume
- Visit-to-email conversion
- Visit-to-scroll-depth on key pages
- CTA click-through rate
- Most-viewed topic cluster
- Waitlist or consultation interest rate
- Return visitor rate

## Technical Constraints

- Must be lightweight and easy to deploy
- Prefer static front-end MVP
- Must support later content expansion
- Should be SEO-friendly
- Should avoid collecting sensitive health data in early stage

## Launch Scope

Launch with one homepage, one lead capture path, and three content pillars.

## Risks

- Positioning may be too broad if it mixes too many health topics
- TCM framing may trigger skepticism if not carefully translated
- Content may become too abstract and fail to convert
- Health claims may drift into compliance risk

## Assumptions

- The first workable audience is domestic Chinese users, not global users
- Trust can be built through modern-language interpretation before any product sale
- A content-first MVP is the fastest low-capital validation path

## User Journey Summary

The user arrives skeptical but curious, sees a familiar health pattern explained in language that respects his intelligence, learns a practical interpretation that includes but does not blindly romanticize TCM, and leaves either with a useful next step or with enough trust to subscribe for more.
